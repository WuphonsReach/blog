--- layout: post title: 'Goon Show: What the Heck Are They Singing?' date: '2002-10-22T16:05:00.000-04:00' author: Wuphon's Reach tags: - '2002' modified_time: '2010-08-04T14:43:39.858-04:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-3097974.post-83366392 blogger_orig_url: http://wuphonsreach.blogspot.com/2002/10/goon-show-what-heck-are-they-singing.shtml --- So anyway, I'm listening to "The Moriarty Murder Mystery" episode (8th series, 17th episode, Jan 20 1958), when Moriarty and Grytpype-Thynne break into a silly singing song around 5 minutes into the sketch (aparently for money since you hear a copper get dropped into their tin mug by a passer-by). Finally, after listening to the song bit a few times (they only sing a few lines before the copper drops and they squable over who gets to hold it) I did a bit of searching on the web and here's what you hear them sing:

Who's kissable, who's lovable?
Miss Annabelle Lee
Who's wonderful, who's marvelous?
Miss Annabelle Lee (my Annabelle Lee)
(copper drops into cup)

This is actually a mangled version of "Miss Annabelle Lee (Who's Wonderful? Who's Marvelous?)" which was written back in circa 1942-1946 by Richman, Sidney Clare, Lew Pollack on the album "Unheard, Vols. 1 & 2: 1942-1946" (lyrics, 2nd version) in that they sing lines 3 and 4 before singing lines 1 and 2.

Well, it's all in the mind you know...
