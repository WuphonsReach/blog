At the start of history, there were 25 civilizations (five each of Dwarves, Elves, Goblins, Humans, Kobolds).  At year 500, one of the Dwarven civs has been wiped out (Bell of Riddling).  Three of the elven civs have been wiped out and only Innocent Hurricane and Fair Boar survive.  Humans and Goblins have fared better and all of their civs have survived to year 500.  Kobolds have done okay.

According to the world_sites_and_pops file:

Those are pretty normal populations for the above world generation settings.  None of the races are close to extinction even if a few civs have been wiped out.

One of the more interesting dwarven civs is the Tool of Waxes, located in the north.



They have never been at war, have founded 18 locations, and only lost 2 of them (both to megabeasts).  What makes them interesting is that according to the trade map, they possibly have trade links to the Wickedness of Thieves goblin civilization.

Here is Wickedness of Thieves:



And the trade map:


The Wereashes Fortress (goblin, bright purple square) by the inland ocean has a link running northeast to the dwarven fortress, Drilledguilds, Interestingly, Drilledguilds has a population listing 108 goblins and 107 dwarves.  According to the site history, both goblins and dwarves have settled there and both have settled, got a job, married, had children.  Some goblins have even migrated to other linked dwarven locations.

So in terms of fortress civilizations, it might be interesting to start as belonging to the Tool of Waxes.
